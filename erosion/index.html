Erosion Issue Report – 2760 Interlaken Drive

We are Thayer and Lori Tate at 2760 Interlaken Drive, and we are reporting an ongoing erosion and runoff issue caused by an uphill neighbor’s yard, which has been disturbed by recent or ongoing construction.

Issue Summary

Due to the construction, the neighbor's yard has turned into a mud and gravel slick. Every time it rains, runoff flows down the sidewalk and the channels on both sides, depositing a significant amount of gravel, mud, and dirt onto our cobblestone driveway.

Observed Impacts

  • Cobblestone is sinking in our driveway, likely due to soil erosion beneath. Our downstream neighbor's cobblestone section, behind the county sewer, has already fallen in the past couple of weeks.
  • Dirt buildup is killing the grass where it rolls off the driveway and into the yard.
  • Loose gravel causes tire spinouts when entering or exiting our driveway—especially for my wife’s vehicle.
  • Gravel is being kicked up onto our vehicles, raising concerns about paint damage and dents.
  • Driveway cleanup is required every two weeks, involving brooming and sometimes heavy effort due to the gravel load.

Steps Already Taken

We have:

  • Spoken to the neighbor and requested installation of a silt fence. He declined and mentioned possibly placing blocks instead. He doesn’t appear motivated to resolve the issue.
  • Contacted our HOA for assistance. They confirmed this falls outside their jurisdiction and directed us to report the issue to the County.
  • Temporarily broomed the top layer of gravel into the street, but refrained from rinsing it into the sewer to preserve visibility for your inspection.
